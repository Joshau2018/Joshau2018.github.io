The Grand TopGolf Expedition

“Success in this game depends less on strength of body than strength of mind and character.”
- Arnold Palmer

This quote was proven to be so true in my golfing expedition. It was the weekend me and a couple of friends decided that we should go to topgolf the following week. One thing led to another and we had a group of 5. We then scheduled to meet up at TopGolf on July 18, 2023, a Tuesday, at 10:30 a.m. We decided on Tuesday, because every Tuesday the time at TopGolf is half-priced. I say, “time,” since TopGolf charges based on the amount of time spent playing there, and not by per person. Soon, I would be embarking on an expedition to TopGolf, not knowing how much it would affect my life.

But first, what is top golf? TopGolf started when two brothers, Steve Joliffe and Dave Joliffe, decided that standard driving ranges are boring, and decided to create a more engaging and trackable way to practice. Thus, they made a high tech game that had sensors that tracked where the distance and direction they went. Eventually, leading to the idea of adding targets to aim at. This was their very first actual game mode called TopGolf. This led to various other game modes such as: Angry Birds, TopScore, and Quick 9. These game modes would eventually be worth billions. TopGolf is an amazing place that offers its players of diverse experience levels a way to enjoy golf and to work on their skills.

The day has arrived, it is now July 18, 2023, the time in central time is nine a.m. The weather is slightly cloudy with the temperature being in the high 80’s. Zakk, the mastermind in producing the idea of going to TopGolf, called me. He told me that his sister is going to come pick me up in fifteen minutes. When they finally arrived at my house Zakk got out to greet me. Zakk had a fresh new hair cut than the last time I saw him. He was wearing black prescription glasses. He had on a black button up shirt with black slacks and Jordan 11’s. Zakk’s height is about 6 foot 3 inches. We spoke for a couple of seconds until I got in the car. I saw Brendon sitting in the back seat eating Hostess Chocolate Donuts. He offered to share. I rejected his offer, however. Brendon was wearing a T-shirt with blue jeans and crocs. Brendon is also tall, 6 foot 2 inches. We then left to go to TopGolf.

After the eventful car ride of Brendon eating donuts, Zakk and his sister dissing each other, and me listening to music we finally made it to TopGolf. We arrived at 9:50 a.m. we went inside where it looked nice and clean. There was a gift shop right where you go inside with button up shirts with the TopGolf logo on them each cost about sixty dollars. The hats were also expensive. Then there was a bar to the left of us. Zakk then walked up to the cashier in the gift shop and asked to buy game time. They told him that there was a 50-minute wait, so we waited in the bar and got some drinks that were non-alcoholic obviously and we passed the time watching TikTok and talking among each other. Eventually, Kayden showed up, sat down, and ordered a drink. Kayden was wearing a white button up shirt with khakis and dress shoes with glasses. Me and Kayden were the only Latinos within the group with Zakk and Brendon both being Anglo-American. We started to talk until we finally got a lobby to play in.

Once we got to the lobby to play, I saw the view from a balcony onto a field with golf balls scattered around and colored circular targets with different rings at various distances, with white being the farthest we could see. All of this was surrounded by a giant black net. After a couple of seconds of taking in the view Zakk imposed a question, “What game mode would y’all want to play.” We ended up deciding on TopScore, a game mode where the further the target is the more points awarded. We each got 20 shots total, so we each did 5 shots at a time.

The first one up was Zakk he chose to use the driver out of a club container provided. Zakk walks over the tape on the floor warning to stand back when someone is swinging and on to the turf with a tee on the left and right. Zakk scanned the golf club on the ball dispenser that was on the right side and a ball shot out a tube and rolled to him. He placed the ball on the right tee, took a couple steps back, aligned the club with the ball, and swung. The ball made a loud crack sound and it curved hard to the right and hit the net. Zakk then got another ball, set it up, aligned himself then swung. This time the ball went straight into white Zakk earned 24 points. Zakk then made three more attempts, scoring an added 16 points bringing his total to 40 points. After Zakk’s turn it was my turn. I picked the same club that Zakk chose, a driver, I placed the ball on the tee lined up my shot then I swung. I completely missed the ball the first time. The second time I hit the ball; however, I barely tipped the ball causing it to travel for a brief moment before it hit the ground. Third time the charm, right? Nope, I did hit the ball, and it did travel a good distance, but the ball curved too far right hitting the net. This is when Brendon started to laugh. This is where I decided to switch clubs since the other one was oversized for my height. This time I was using the women's golf club that was provided. I walked up, set the ball up, positioned myself then I swung. The ball could easily be seen going straight into the second ring of the green target putting 8 points on the board for me. With the last one I tried to replicate the same hit just with more power. This is more difficult than hitting the ball, normally considering it went ten yards before landing. This was where Zakk told me, “If you relax you will actually be able to hit the ball further than if you are trying to force it.” After my turn it was Kayden’s turn, he did pretty well and got 24 points. Finally, it was Brendon's turn. It was pretty funny he missed the ball five times back-to-back on his first ball. Me and Kayden were making fun of him and laughing at him. Although that would be short lived because when Brendon finally made contact with the ball it went straight in the middle of brown giving him 36 points. Although after this hit he would go pointless for a while. Then, it was Zakk’s turn he kind of choked by managing to miss every target either by barely bouncing over it or going straight into the net. Now it was my turn with Zakk's wisdom in mind. I relaxed and focused on hitting the ball. I didn’t aim at a particular target, I was just zoned in on hitting the ball and somehow, I managed to hit it in the blue target’s second ring thanks to a lucky bounce, giving me a total of 24 points. My next four hits however would avoid the target. Kayden’s turn would go slightly worse than before, only bringing his score up to 30 points. Brandon's first hit was probably a fluke, because he managed not to get a single point. We did not laugh though because of karma. Zakk’s next five would be amazing, making four out of five in a target. By the end of this turn he would have a total of 78 points. Next it would be my turn to make only two of these shots giving me a total of 40. Kayden’s turn arrived. He made one shot bringing his total to 42 points. Brandon only made one shot making his total 36. Only Zakk would make a shot on the last turn bringing him to a total that I rather not say. We would play several more games of various game modes such as an Angry Birds game mode where Brandon would win, because he set his difficulty too easy when the rest of us were on hard mode.

As we played more games, I gradually became more relaxed and comfortable with golfing. As a result, my performance improved, which in turn caused me to find myself experiencing more joy with friends. This lesson at TopGolf can also be applied to other scenarios; it applies to scenarios such as taking an exam, where the more relaxed I am the more information I can remember, or when I am playing a video game, where with a calm state of mind my performance is at its best.

Through this experience, my ideology has undergone a transition. I shifted from believing that I should be serious all the time to believing that a relaxed state of mind is instrumental to enjoy life to the fullest. I also learned a valuable lesson: I should never challenge Zakk to a competitive game of TopGolf, because he will undoubtedly win. The grand expedition to TopGolf has shifted to more of a leisurely excursion at TopGolf.
